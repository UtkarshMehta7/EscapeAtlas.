History of Nainital

Nainital, a beautiful hill station located in the northern Indian state of Uttarakhand, is known for its picturesque beauty and rich history. The town is nestled around the Naini Lake, which is believed to be the spot where the eye of Goddess Sati fell, giving the lake its name.

The town was discovered in 1841 by a British official, Mr. P. Barron, who recognized the potential of the region as a summer retreat. Over the years, Nainital has evolved into a popular destination for tourists from all over the world.

Popular Tourist Attractions

Naini Lake

The Naini Lake is the heart of Nainital. It offers a beautiful boating experience with stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can enjoy paddle boating, rowing, or simply take a peaceful walk around the lake.

Naina Devi Temple

Situated at the northern shore of Naini Lake, the Naina Devi Temple is dedicated to Goddess Naina Devi. It is one of the most revered shrines in Nainital, attracting thousands of devotees every year.

Tiffin Top

Tiffin Top, also known as Dorothy's Seat, is a vantage point offering panoramic views of Nainital and its surrounding hills. It is a popular spot for trekkers and nature enthusiasts.

Things to Do in Nainital

Nainital is a perfect place for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts. Apart from the usual sightseeing, here are some activities you can enjoy:

  • Trekking: There are several trekking trails around Nainital, including the trek to Snow View Point and Kilbury.
  • Shopping: Visit Mall Road for local handicrafts, woolens, and souvenirs.
  • Boating: Spend a serene afternoon on Naini Lake, enjoying a peaceful boat ride.
  • Visit the Zoo: The Nainital Zoo is home to a variety of wildlife, including the Himalayan Black Bear and the Siberian Tiger.

Plan Your Trip to Nainital

Nainital is well connected by road, rail, and air. The nearest railway station is Kathgodam, about 35 km away, and the nearest airport is Pantnagar, about 70 km away.

The best time to visit Nainital is during the summer months (March to June) when the weather is pleasant and cool. Winter (November to February) is perfect for snow lovers, while the monsoon season should be avoided due to the possibility of landslides.
